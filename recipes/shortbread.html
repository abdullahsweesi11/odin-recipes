Shortbread Recipe

A very simple biscuit ('cookie' for North Americans) recipe, yet really delicious!

The fundamental trio of ingredients—butter, sugar, and flour—harmonizes to produce a tasty treat.

Originating in Scotland, this classic delicacy is celebrated for its simplicity and yet boasts an unparalleled richness and crumbly texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups butter
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 4 ½ cups all-purpose flour

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. All ingredients gathered to make Scottish shortbreads.
  3. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
  4. Beat butter and brown sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  5. Butter and brown sugar creamed together.
  6. Add 3 to 3 3/4 cups flour; mix well.
  7. Flour added, mixing well.
  8. Sprinkle board with remaining flour. Knead for 5 minutes, adding enough flour to make a soft dough.
  9. Dough kneaded with some flour to make a soft dough.
  10. Roll out dough to 1/2-inch thickness. Cut into 3x1-inch strips.
  11. Dough rolled out to appropriate thickness and cut into strips.
  12. Prick shortbread cookies with a fork and place on ungreased baking sheets.
  13. Strips placed on a baking sheet and pricked with a fork.
  14. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are golden brown, 20 to 25 minutes. Cool on the baking sheet briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
